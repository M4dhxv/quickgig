02 — Brand Foundation

Why we exist

The job hunt was never built for frontline workers. It was built for office résumés and HR portals — endless forms, "we'll be in touch," listings already filled by the time you find them.

GigNearby flips it. Upload your CV once — or just tell us what you do — and we read the live market every minute, find the matches near you, and the second one lands, your phone buzzes on WhatsApp. No scrolling. No forms. No waiting your turn.

Because for working people, getting there first isn't a luxury. It's the difference between the shift and the "sorry, just filled."

Mission

Get working people to good work near home, faster than anyone else — zero friction, zero gatekeeping.

04 — Color

Palette

Cobalt#2E5BFFLocation · links
Ink Navy#0E1633Text · dark fields
Slate#5A6178Muted text
Haze#F5F6FALight tint

Signal Orange is the brand — fills, icons, CTAs, the "ping." Accessibility: orange on white is ~3.2:1, so reserve it for large/bold text, fills, and buttons (white labels); use Orange Deep #E8430A for stricter contrast. Small body copy is Ink Navy. Cobalt is for links and "near you" UI. Never use green/emerald — that's GigGrab.

09 — Do & Don't

Keep it GigNearby

Do lead with proximity + speed — "near you," "first."
Do keep CTAs Signal Orange and singular.
Do use real worker photography with a diverse cast.
Do show live numbers in Space Mono — "1,284 jobs near you."
Do write like a sharp friend who respects their time.
Don't use green/emerald anywhere — that's GigGrab.
Don't call it a "job board" or say "post jobs."
Don't use orange for small body text.
Don't use corporate stock imagery or glass offices.
Don't make the worker fill out anything they don't have to.
