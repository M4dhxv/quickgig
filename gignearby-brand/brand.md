## Why this identity (and why it's NOT GigGrab)

GigGrab (the employer-side app) is calm, emerald, SaaS. GigNearby is the **worker's** app — it should feel like energy in your pocket, not a dashboard. So the system is built on **Signal Orange**: the colour of a notification, of hi-vis workwear, of "you're first to know." Paired with a **Cobalt** that reads as the map's "you are here" dot, and an **Ink Navy** that grounds it in trust. Bold **Archivo** headlines replace neutral grotesks. Nothing here collides with GigGrab — different colour, different type, different feeling.

## Positioning

GigNearby is the job app built for how frontline work actually gets filled: **fast, local, first-come.** No cover letters, no forms typed 40 times. Upload your CV once (or none — we'll build your profile), and GigNearby does the looking — warehouse, care, hospitality, logistics, retail, cleaning, security, delivery — then **pings you on WhatsApp the instant a nearby match appears.**

**The wedge:** Job boards make workers chase. GigNearby brings the work to you, nearby, and gets you there *first*. Speed + proximity is the whole promise — both are in the name.

**What we are not:** not a job board you scroll, not an application portal, not a staffing agency that ghosts you. No forms, no fees to start.

### Audience

- **Primary** — US frontline / deskless workers (18–45) who change jobs often for better pay or hours. Phone-first, time-poor, allergic to forms.
- **Secondary** — Multilingual newcomers (32 languages) who find English portals hostile; shift workers (parents, students, carers) who need work *close to home*.
- **Anchor persona** — **Marcus, 26, Houston.** Drives for a 3PL warehouse, switches jobs every ~8 months for a dollar more an hour. Lives on his phone, hates re-typing applications, hears about better gigs two weeks too late. GigNearby pings him the closest one first.

## Mission

Get working people to good work near home, faster than anyone else — with zero friction and zero gatekeeping.

## Values

1. **Workers first, always.** Free to start. No fees to get hired.
2. **Respect the hustle.** Their time is money. No forms, no busywork, no jargon.
3. **Close to home counts.** A short commute changes a whole life. Proximity is a feature, not a filter.
4. **First, not eventually.** Speed is dignity. If a job's out there, you should be the first to know.
5. **Everyone's welcome.** 32 languages. No account to start.

## Brand story (why this exists)

The job hunt was never built for frontline workers. It was built for office résumés and HR portals — endless forms, "we'll be in touch," listings already filled by the time you find them. If you stack pallets, drive routes, change beds, or work a register, you've lived it.

GigNearby flips it. Upload your CV once — or just tell us what you do — and we read the live market every minute, find the matches near you, and the second one lands, your phone buzzes on WhatsApp. No scrolling, no forms, no waiting your turn. Just the closest jobs, first.

Because for working people, getting there first isn't a luxury. It's the difference between the shift and the "sorry, just filled."

## Voice & tone

**Adjectives:** plain-spoken · fast · warm · confident · respectful · zero-fluff. Talk like a sharp friend who's got your back and won't waste your time. Short sentences. Real words. Celebrate the win without hype.

**Copy examples**
- **Headline:** The closest jobs, first.
- **Sub:** Upload your CV once. We'll ping you the moment a job near you opens up.
- **Button:** Find work near me
- **Button (alt):** Get job alerts on WhatsApp
- **WhatsApp alert:** New warehouse job 2 miles away — $19/hr. You're first to know. Tap to see it.
- **Empty state:** No matches yet — we're watching. The second a job near you drops, your phone buzzes.
- **Error:** That didn't go through. Not you — us. Give it another tap.
- **Success:** You're in. Sit tight — we'll ping you the closest jobs first.
- **Social:** Stop scrolling job boards. Upload once, get the closest gigs pinged to you. Free. gignearby.com

**Forbidden:** "synergy," "empower," "unlock your potential," "dream job," "world-class," "seamless," "leverage," "elevate," "best-in-class," "passionate about," "revolutionary," HR-speak, "job board," "post a job," exclamation-mark stacking.

**Pairings:** Signal Orange CTAs with white labels (large/bold) — use Orange Deep for stricter contrast. Cobalt for links, location chips, "near you" UI. White/Haze on Ink Navy for dark sections. **Accessibility:** Orange on white is ~3.2:1 — large/bold text, fills, and buttons only; small body text is Ink Navy. Cobalt on white passes AA. White on Ink Navy passes everywhere.
**Never:** orange small body text · orange + cobalt as equal large fields (one leads) · more than one orange CTA per view · amber/green (those read as GigGrab).

## Typography

- **Display — Archivo** (800/900). Headlines, wordmark, big stats-as-words. Bold, confident, contemporary. Tight tracking (-0.02em) at large sizes.
  `https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Archivo:wght@500;600;700;800;900&display=swap`
- **Body — Hanken Grotesk** (400/500/600). UI, paragraphs, labels. Extremely legible on phones.
  `https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Hanken+Grotesk:wght@400;500;600;700&display=swap`
- **Stats — Space Mono** (400/700). Job counts, pay, distance ("2.1 mi," "$19/hr," "1,284 jobs") — gives a live-ticker/alert feel.
  `https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Space+Mono:wght@400;700&display=swap`

**Hierarchy (px):** H1 60/1.0 Archivo 900 · H2 40/1.05 Archivo 800 · H3 26/1.15 Archivo 700 · Lead 20/1.5 Hanken 500 · Body 16/1.6 Hanken 400 · Small 14/1.5 Hanken 500 · Caption 12/1.4 Hanken 600 uppercase 0.1em · Stat 24 Space Mono 700. **Min body on mobile: 16px.**

## Logo

- **Symbol** — a bold rounded **map pin** (Signal Orange) with a white "you are here" dot, and a **Cobalt ping** radiating off the upper-right (the alert — "you're first to know"). Personifies *nearby + pinged first.* Favicon variant = pin + dot only (drop the ping for 16px). File: `logo/symbol.svg`, app icon `logo/symbol-app-icon.svg`.
- **Wordmark** — "GigNearby" set in Archivo Black (900), tracking -0.02em, one word, capital N. Color: Ink Navy, or white on dark, or Signal Orange on Navy.
- **Lockup** — symbol left of wordmark, gap = 0.5× cap height; stacked centers the symbol above.
- **Clear space:** one pin-width all sides. **Min:** symbol 16px; wordmark 90px wide.

### Logo don'ts
Never stretch/condense · never rotate the pin · never recolor outside the palette (especially never emerald — that's GigGrab) · never add drop shadows or gradients · never place the orange mark on a busy photo without a scrim or chip · never outline the wordmark.

## Iconography

Geometric line icons · 2px stroke · round caps/joins · 24px grid · ~4px corner radius. Files in `icons/`. Beyond the set, use **Lucide** at 2px.

## Photography

Documentary, candid, real frontline workers in real settings (docks, care homes, sites, cafes, vans), usually checking a phone, not posed. Natural light, slight film grain, shallow depth. **Diverse cast always.** Hopeful, dignified, everyday. Forbidden: studio strobes, suits at laptops, handshakes, white-void stock, AI-smooth skin. See `prompts/photography.md`.

## Visual world

Sunlit, in-motion, neighborhood. Phones in hands. Hi-vis, scrubs, aprons, hard hats. Maps + pins + the cobalt "ping" as motif. Orange as the spark of the alert. The feeling: *the good gig is close, and it just buzzed your phone.*

## Touchpoints

Phone in hand showing the app · WhatsApp job-alert message · die-cut pin sticker on a hard hat / bottle · cotton tote · break-room flyer. App icon = Ink-Navy rounded square + orange pin + cobalt ping. Favicon = pin only.

## Do & Don't

**Do** — lead with proximity + speed ("near you," "first") · keep CTAs Signal Orange and singular · use real worker photography with a diverse cast · show live numbers in Space Mono · write like a sharp friend.
**Don't** — use emerald/green anywhere (that's GigGrab) · call it a "job board" or "post jobs" · use orange for small body text · stack hype · make the worker fill out anything they don't have to.
